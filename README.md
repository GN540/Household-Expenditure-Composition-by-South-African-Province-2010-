Household Expenditure Composition by South African Province (2010)
Type: Composition analysis | Tools: Excel/Google Sheets | Data source: ALX Africa 
1. The question: 
South African household spending is often assumed to follow a simple pattern: necessities first (housing, utilities), then everything else. Does that hold true across all nine provinces, or are there exceptions worth flagging?
2. The data

Household expenditure (Rand millions, 2010) across three tracked categories, housing/water/electricity, transport, and recreation/culture, for each of South Africa's nine provinces.
Note: this dataset tracks three expenditure categories, not full household budgets. All composition percentages below are calculated as a share of these three categories combined, not as a share of total household spend. This is stated explicitly so the chart isn't misread as "% of total budget."

3. Process

- Cleaned and restructured the raw table into a long format suitable for charting 
- Converted absolute Rand values to percentage-of-basket per province, since raw Rand totals are driven by population/province size and would obscure the actual spending mix, which is what a composition chart should show
- Built a 100%-stacked bar chart in Excel, one bar per province, sorted to make outliers visible
- Cross-checked every claimed insight against the underlying numbers before finalizing

4. Key insights

- Housing dominates the basket in most provinces. In 7 of 9 provinces, housing/water/electricity is the largest of the three tracked categories, ranging from ~55% (Northern Cape) to ~65% (Free State) of the basket.
- Western Cape and Gauteng break the pattern; transport edges out housing. Western Cape spends 50.1% of this basket on transport vs. 45.7% on housing, the largest reversal in the dataset. Gauteng shows the same reversal but by a much narrower margin (48.7% vs 47.6%). (Earlier draft of this analysis attributed this only to Gauteng;  re-checking the numbers showed Western Cape is actually the stronger example of the two.)
- Recreation and culture is consistently the smallest share everywhere, never exceeding ~4.2% of the basket in any province. This is worth noting as a limitation of the dataset (recreation spend may be undercounted or narrowly defined) as much as a finding about household priorities.
